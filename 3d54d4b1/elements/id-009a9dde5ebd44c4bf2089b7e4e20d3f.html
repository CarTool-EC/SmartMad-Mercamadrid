MQTT ()
Definition: MQTT (Message Queuing Telemetry Transport) is a lightweight messaging protocol designed for efficient communication between devices with limited bandwidth or resources. It follows a publish-subscribe model, where devices (clients) publish messages to a broker, and other devices subscribe to receive relevant messages. MQTT is widely used in Internet of Things (IoT) applications due to its low power and bandwidth requirements, making it ideal for real-time data exchange in environments with limited connectivity.
