National Binding Instruments ()
Definition: National Binding Instruments (NBI) is a grouping that covers a broad range of legislative and regulatory frameworks related to interoperability in various domains. This includes structural, behavioural, and governance interoperability. The NBI provides a structured approach to the implementation and management of these frameworks, ensuring that they are in line with national and international standards and best practices. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The National Binding Instruments (NBI) catalogue is a comprehensive repository of legislative and regulatory frameworks that govern interoperability in various domains. It includes the Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI), which covers the National Schema on eIdentification, Cybersecurity, eDelivery, and access to Base Registries. It also includes the Legislation on Behavioural Interoperability (NBI) and the Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NBI). Each of these categories contains specific elements that provide detailed information on the respective legislative and regulatory frameworks. The NBI catalogue is designed to facilitate the implementation and management of these frameworks, ensuring compliance with national and international standards and best practices. Example: For instance, under the Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NBI), there are specific elements such as the National Schema on eIdentification which provides guidelines on electronic identification processes. Similarly, the National Schema on Cybersecurity outlines the legislative framework for managing cybersecurity risks. The National Schema on eDelivery provides the regulatory framework for electronic delivery services, while the National Schema on the access to Base Registries outlines the guidelines for accessing base registries. These elements provide a structured approach to implementing and managing interoperability in their respective domains.
