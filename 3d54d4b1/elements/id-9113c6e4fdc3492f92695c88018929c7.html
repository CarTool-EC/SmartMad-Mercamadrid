SMDS Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NBI) ()
Definition: Tax Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NBI) is a grouping that covers a wide range of elements related to national tax legislation, schemas, and acts. These elements encompass various aspects of tax law, including tributary court cases, income-related taxes, mobile assets, carbon-related taxes, company classification rules, deductible taxes on loans and mortgages, taxes related to property, religious community fees, criminal assets, social tax, tax fraud and evasion, unemployment insurance, VAT, tax procedures, and property tax. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The elements within the Tax Legislation on Governance Interoperability (NBI) catalogue are designed to provide a comprehensive overview of national tax legislation and related schemas. They cover a broad spectrum of tax-related topics, from specific types of taxes (income, property, VAT, etc.) to legal procedures and strategies for combating tax fraud. Each element represents a distinct aspect of tax law, providing a detailed picture of the tax landscape and facilitating interoperability in governance. Example: For instance, the 'National Schema on tributary court cases' element would contain information about the legal framework and procedures for handling tax-related court cases at the national level. Similarly, the 'National Schema on Co2 (carbon) related taxes' would detail the legislation and regulations surrounding carbon taxation. The 'Legislation on the deductible tax es on loans and mortgages' would provide information on how these types of taxes can be deducted under national law.
