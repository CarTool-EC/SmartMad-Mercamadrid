Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ()
Definition: Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ABB is a grouping that covers the legal and regulatory framework related to the structural interoperability of systems, networks, and services. It includes laws, regulations, directives, and other legal instruments that govern the design, implementation, and operation of interoperable systems and services. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The NNBI ABB is a comprehensive catalogue of legal and regulatory instruments that are relevant to structural interoperability. It provides a structured and systematic approach to understanding and navigating the complex legal landscape of interoperability. The catalogue includes a wide range of elements, each with a specific name and description. These elements represent different aspects of the legal and regulatory framework for interoperability, including specific laws and regulations, overarching legal principles, regulatory authorities and their roles, compliance requirements, and enforcement mechanisms. The NNBI ABB is an essential resource for policymakers, regulators, industry professionals, and other stakeholders involved in the design, implementation, and operation of interoperable systems and services. Example: Examples of application of the Legislation on Structural Interoperability (NNBI) ABB could include the development of a new digital communication network that needs to comply with specific interoperability standards, or a public administration implementing a new e-government service that needs to be compatible with existing systems and services. In these cases, the NNBI ABB would provide a comprehensive overview of the relevant legal and regulatory requirements, helping to ensure compliance and avoid potential legal and regulatory pitfalls.
