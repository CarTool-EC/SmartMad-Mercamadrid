Data Act Proposal ()
Description: The Digital Services Act is a regulation passed by the European Parliament and Council that aims to create a single market for digital services in the European Union. The act amends Directive 2000/31/EC and introduces new rules for online platforms, including requirements for transparency, accountability, and content moderation. It also establishes a new regulatory framework for digital services, including provisions for data protection, cybersecurity, and consumer protection. The act is designed to promote innovation and competition in the digital economy while ensuring that online platforms operate in a responsible and accountable manner.
