Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ()
Definition: Legislation on Structural Interoperability (ENBI) ABB is a grouping that covers a range of legislative documents and recommendations from the European Union. These documents aim to guide and regulate the implementation of interoperability within the digital space, addressing issues such as disinformation, digital rights and principles, and the creation of a secure European e-ID. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The ENBI catalogue includes key documents such as the 'European Interoperability Framework - Implementation Strategy', which outlines the Commission's strategy for implementing interoperability across the EU. It also includes the 'Strengthened Code of Practice on Disinformation', a document aimed at combating the spread of false information online. The 'Declaration for the Future of Internet' and 'Declaration on Digital Rights and Principles' set out the EU's vision for a free, open and secure internet, while the 'Recommendation on Building a Joint Cyber Unit' and 'Recommendation on a trusted and secure European e-ID' provide specific recommendations for enhancing cybersecurity and digital identity within the EU. Example: An example of the application of the ENBI catalogue could be a European digital service provider looking to ensure their services are in line with EU regulations and recommendations. They could consult the 'European Interoperability Framework - Implementation Strategy' for guidance on implementing interoperable systems, and the 'Recommendation on a trusted and secure European e-ID' for advice on creating secure digital identities for their users.
